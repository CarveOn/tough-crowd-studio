03Phase 0 · Setup

From a blank Mac to a working studio brain.

About 15 minutes, no technical skill needed. Claude Code comes as a friendly desktop app for Mac, a normal window where you chat in plain English and watch it work on the files and services on your Mac. That is where you live. A Terminal exists underneath for the rare one-off, but day to day you never open it.

Set up the app, step by step

Download the Claude Code desktop app

Go to claude.com/claude-code and download the Mac app. Open the downloaded file and drag Claude Code into your Applications folder, exactly like installing any other Mac app. No Terminal, no developer tools, nothing technical.

Open it and sign in

Launch the app and click Sign in. Use your Claude Pro account ($20/mo), the same login as the Claude website. Pro is a flat, predictable fee and far cheaper than pay-per-use for daily work. Move up to Max only if you ever hit limits.

Stay on the subscription

Just sign in with your Claude account inside the app. Do not paste an API key in anywhere, that quietly switches you to pricier pay-per-use billing.

Point it at a folder

In the app choose Open project (or New project) and pick a folder on your Mac, for example one called tough-crowd on your Desktop. That folder becomes the workspace for this work. Each client gets its own folder later.

Start talking

Type what you want in the chat box, just like messaging the Claude app, and it does the work and shows you what changed. That is the whole loop, and you do it all in a normal window, never a terminal.

If you ever do need the Terminal

You almost never will. A handful of one-off jobs are quickest as a single pasted line, and when that happens the app can run it for you, or you open Terminal (Cmd+Space, type Terminal) and paste. The one line you might ever need, as an alternative to the app installer above, is below. Skip it unless something specifically asks for it.

Terminal · only if needed
curl -fsSL https://claude.ai/install.sh | bash

The single most important file you'll create

CLAUDE.md is a plain text file the app reads automatically every time you open the project, and treats as standing instructions. Think of it as the studio's operating brain. You do not type it out yourself; just ask Claude in the app to create it, then refine it. Write down once what you would otherwise repeat forever: your rates, your hidden-hours rule, your voice, your folders, your do/don't rules. The quality of everything Claude makes for you rises and falls on this file. The critic flagged it as the cheapest, highest-leverage thing in the whole plan, and it costs nothing.

Paste into the app and say "save this as CLAUDE.md" — refine over time
# Tough Crowd Studio — operating brain

## Who we are

## Brand voice (all written output)
- UK English. No em dashes, ever.
- Confident, sharp, no hype, no filler. Premium and human.
- Never sound like first-generation AI output.

- Each client folder has its own CLAUDE.md with that client's brief and voice.

## Do / Don't
- DO explain what you're about to do in plain English before you do it.
- DO warn me before anything that deletes, overwrites or goes live.
- DON'T send any email automatically. Draft only. I send.
- DON'T deploy to the live site without asking. DON'T invent client facts.

How it all connects

MCP connectors

The standard way Claude plugs into outside services, like a USB port for software. One plug for Cloudflare, one for Google Drive, and so on. You add them in the app's settings, or just ask Claude to connect one, then approve in your browser.

Skills & subagents

A Skill is a saved set of instructions for a task you repeat. A subagent is a specialist Claude hands a job to. You don't need either on day one. Add them when you catch yourself repeating instructions.

Cloudflare ↔ Claude

The official Cloudflare plugin installs the connector and the deploy tool together, so you can say "deploy the latest site" or "add a DNS record" and Claude does it.

Safety, while you build confidence

The app asks for your approval before it edits a file or runs anything. Stay on that default. The key tool is Plan Mode, a toggle in the app: Claude writes out exactly what it would do and changes nothing until you click approve. Use it for anything expensive to get wrong, going live, deleting, payments, DNS.

It tells the client you intend to scope the work to what they can actually spend, instead of designing something they can't afford. Wire it up

Set the form to drop a row into Google Sheets and fire a Resend confirmation to the enquirer plus an alert to you. Keep it short. Every extra field costs you a serious enquiry.

Hosting it on Cloudflare

Cloudflare is both your DNS (the phone book that points the domain at the site) and your host, which is why custom domain and SSL setup is almost fully automatic once the domain is in your account.

Create a free Cloudflare account and add the domain

Sign up at dash.cloudflare.com, turn on two-factor. Easiest route for a fresh start: register toughcrowd.studio directly through Cloudflare, where it arrives pre-wired for DNS and SSL. No card needed for the free tier.

Deploy in one step (you just ask the app)

You'll just say "deploy the latest site" in the chat. Under the hood it's wrangler, Cloudflare's deploy tool, but Claude runs it. Each push is a new versioned deployment you can roll back, so it's safe to repeat as often as you like.

What Claude runs for you

npx wrangler pages deploy ./out --project-name toughcrowd-studio

Connect the domain

In Workers & Pages, open the project, Custom domains, add toughcrowd.studio. Cloudflare adds the DNS record and SSL certificate for you in the background. A one-time click, then forget it.

Cost: effectively zero

Cloudflare Pages free tier is unusually generous: unlimited bandwidth and requests, 500 builds a month, 20,000 files. For a solo studio marketing site, expect to pay nothing, possibly ever. The ~£50–55/mo figure elsewhere is for the separate Studio app, not this site.

Deposits & Stripe fees

Two Stripe Payment Links per project: 50% at signature, 50% on delivery. UK card fee is 1.5% + 20p per transaction; EEA cards 2.5% + 20p, international 3.25% + 20p (verified June 2026). On a £5,000 deposit from a UK client that's roughly £75. Build it into your rate, never surface it to the client.

The critic's flag: do not skip this

The plan jumps from proposal to deposit. Put a signed scope between them. The unsigned-scope gap is where free revisions, scope creep and non-payment live. A simple terms-plus-scope doc with e-sign (or a countersigned PDF logged in Supabase), paired with the deposit so signature and money happen together. And a proper UK VAT invoice, not just a payment link, so your accountant can reconcile it.

The honest risk

This system makes pricing fast, not accurate. If the Sheet's hours are wishful, the figure drifts from reality and you quietly underprice premium work. Audit the Sheet against actual project effort every quarter. That part stays your judgement.

The critic's addition: don't stop at a list of leads. The numbers worth surfacing are win rate by lead source and by budget band, and average deal size. Those tell you which enquiries to chase and which to decline. For a premium positioning play, a healthy decline-and-still-win rate is the proof the strategy works. Capacity, so you don't overcommit

Set a normal working day, block out the real-life stuff (school pickups, holidays, days off), and the app shows remaining available hours by day and by week. It feeds the "free this week" number on the dashboard, and it's the honest answer to "can I take this on?".

Deadline alerts, automated

Reuse the exact amber/red thresholds from the app to fire internal emails to you on a daily Resend cron: "AMBER: Bó tone of voice due in 2 days", "OVERDUE: Acme logo pack, 1 day late". The subject carries the state so it's scannable on your phone. One sender, one recipient, no client ever sees it. Lock the cron endpoint behind a secret so nobody else can trigger it.

Build the easy 80% first

One brand record, two filtered views (a clean shareable version and a detailed internal one), never two documents that drift. Colour palette with copy buttons, typography with licences, do/don't, asset library: all structured content rendered nicely, well within Claude Code's range over a few sessions.

The "one SVG, every output" idea: real, but phase two

Feasible with sharp (a fast Node image library). Sizes are easy. Colour variants need cleanly authored SVGs. And the classic trap: if your logo SVG uses live text, the renderer may drop the font, so outline all text to paths first. Build it as a standalone script you run on client work before wiring it into the app. Hold this line: it's a margin-improver later, never a launch blocker.

Why this is the real prize

Your platform cost is flat at ~£50–55/mo regardless of how many client brands you host, because they share one app. Charge even £20–40/mo per hosted brand and everything after the first few is near pure margin. It lands best with SME and founder brands, not enterprise (who often run their own systems). Frame it as keeping the brand live and governed, the visible proof the work is human-judged, not as software.

Must

Automated database backups + a tested restore

Your entire commercial memory sits in one Postgres instance. A weekly automated export to Google Drive plus a restore you've actually tested once. Cheap insurance.

Should

Aggregate profitability, not just per-job

Effective rate over time, by service, by client. That's what tells you whether to raise prices, drop a service or fire a client. Same data, one extra view.

Should

A brand-guardianship retainer, designed now

Recurring revenue is what makes a solo studio bankable and smooths the feast-or-famine. Define and price the offer now, even if you build the tooling later.

Should

A one-page GDPR / privacy notice

Under UK GDPR you store client data on US-hosted services. You don't need a compliance department, but a privacy notice and a data line in your terms protect the premium credibility.

Should

Testimonial & case-study capture at handover

Bake the ask into the final-delivery email so it never gets skipped. For a studio selling proof of taste, this is direct marketing fuel that grows the site itself.

Later

Referral capture + a light client portal

Referral is the dominant channel for premium B2B branding and costs nothing. A read-only client status view kills the "where are we?" emails. Both after the core is stable.

Ship a site that takes qualified, budget-led enquiries and a clean proposal-to-signed-deposit flow before you build anything else. The Studio app is a margin and sanity tool, not the business. The business is winning premium work on the strength of judgement. Build the earning engine first, instrument it second.
